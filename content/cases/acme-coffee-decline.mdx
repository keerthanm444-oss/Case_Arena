Revenue is flat, so the question is cost — but be disciplined: check both sides anyway. A revenue mix shift toward lower-margin items would *also* look like flat revenue + falling profit.

### Where to dig first

In Acme's situation:

- **Variable costs** — coffee beans, milk, cups — have seen real input-price inflation over the last 2 years. Probably the biggest single driver.
- **Labor** — wage pressure especially for shift staff. Check whether stores have absorbed that into ticket prices.
- **Real estate** — fixed rent steps tend to move up at renewal. If renewals clustered into the period in question, that explains a meaningful chunk.

### Recommendation skeleton

1. **Diagnose** which of the three drivers above is largest — get last quarter's P&L by line item.
2. **Address inputs** Acme can control: re-bid bean contracts, sequence the rent renewals.
3. **Address inputs** Acme can't directly control: take small, frequent price increases on the highest-margin SKUs to absorb cost pressure without losing traffic.

## What judges look for

- Did you ask about **store-level vs. company-level** dynamics? You need both.
- Did you ask about **time horizon** before quantifying anything?
- Did you propose a **specific** action (re-bid contracts) rather than a vague one ("reduce costs")?
