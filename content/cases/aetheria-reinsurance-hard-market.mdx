## The situation

Reinsurance is a highly cyclical industry with multi-year "hard" and "soft" market cycles. Hard markets — driven by catastrophe losses, capital depletion across the industry, or both — typically last 2-4 years before competitive capital re-enters. The strategic challenge is capturing the hard-market opportunity without compromising underwriting discipline.

Historical experience: reinsurers that grow aggressively in hard markets often outperform short-term but underperform over the cycle because they accumulate poorly-priced business at the end of the cycle that becomes a drag in the subsequent soft market.

Three branches:

1. **Line-by-line market conditions** — property cat (current rates +50-80% from 2018), casualty (+25-40%), specialty (varies). Different growth strategies per line.
2. **Capital deployment options** — same-cycle growth (deploy now), through-cycle approach (grow modestly, retain capital for soft-market opportunities), defensive (return capital to shareholders if not deployed productively).
3. **Risk discipline** — at what point does rate increase justify rate increase? Modeled loss costs are the floor.

## Key analysis

The diagnostic question: **at current rates, what's the expected combined ratio for new business by line?**

A typical pattern in a hardening market:

| Line | Current rate level | Expected combined ratio | Action |
|------|---------------------|--------------------------|--------|
| Property cat (high-frequency US) | +60% from 5yr avg | 75-85% | **Grow aggressively** |
| Property cat (lower-frequency global) | +40% | 80-90% | Grow selectively |
| Casualty (general liability) | +30% | 95-105% | Maintain, no growth |
| Casualty (D&O, professional) | +50% | 88-95% | Grow selectively |
| Specialty (aviation, marine) | +25% | 92-98% | Hold |

This decomposition shows that "hard market" is not uniform. Property cat at +60% rates is genuinely attractive; casualty at +30% may still be inadequate given social inflation.

The non-obvious move: **grow only where the expected combined ratio is materially below industry-typical hard-market levels** (75-85%). Anything above that is closer to fair-priced — not the opportunity the headline rate suggests.

## Recommendation skeleton

**Recommendation:** **Selective growth — primarily in property cat.** Increase property cat capacity by 25-35% in the upcoming renewal (deploy $400-600M additional capital). Hold casualty and specialty flat. Maintain $200-300M capital reserve for through-cycle deployment. Target expected combined ratio on new business: <85%.

**Three supports:**

1. Property cat is the line where rate increases meaningfully exceed expected loss-cost trajectories.
2. Selective approach maintains underwriting discipline that has historically separated outperformers from cycle-average performers.
3. Capital reserve provides flexibility for opportunistic deployment if the market continues to harden or to support soft-market positioning later.

**One risk:** if a major catastrophe occurs during the renewal cycle, capacity needs may surge and Aetheria's modest growth may underperform peers' aggressive growth. Mitigate by maintaining strong client relationships now — favored counterparty status during a stressed renewal lets us choose business rather than chase it.

## What judges look for

- Did you reject **uniform hard-market growth** in favor of line-by-line analysis?
- Did you size **expected combined ratio at current rates** as the decision criterion?
- Did you identify **property cat as the most attractive line** based on rate-vs-loss-cost analysis?
- Did you propose **capital reserve** for through-cycle deployment rather than full deployment?
