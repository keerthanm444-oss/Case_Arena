## The situation

Indie beauty brand acquisitions have been one of the most active categories in CPG M&A over the past decade. Multiples have ranged from 3-8x revenue depending on growth, brand strength, and competitive dynamics. Many acquisitions have been successful (Dollar Shave Club, Drunk Elephant); many have been disappointing (acquired brands losing growth + relevance post-deal).

The pattern of failure: large parent companies extract cost synergies but inadvertently kill the brand attributes that made the acquisition attractive — founder voice, indie credibility, agile product development.

Three branches:

1. **Strategic fit** — does this brand fill a real portfolio gap (Gen-Z, category, channel)? Or is it acquired because it's "hot"?
2. **Valuation** — is $600M (7.5x revenue) defensible? At 12-15% EBITDA margin (typical for indie beauty at this scale), implied EBITDA multiple is 40-50x.
3. **Integration risk** — can Amalfi acquire without killing the brand? Specific consideration: founder retention, autonomous operating model, channel preservation.

## Key analysis

### Strategic fit

Gen-Z skincare consumer is increasingly important and underserved by traditional beauty conglomerates. If Amalfi lacks Gen-Z credible brands, this acquisition addresses a real gap. Distribution synergies (Amalfi's global retail relationships + indie brand's product) can be meaningful — typically 20-40% growth uplift in years 2-3 from accelerated international + retail expansion.

### Valuation

7.5x revenue at 40% growth and 12% margin = expensive but in range for hot indie beauty. The math:
- Year 5 revenue projection at 25% sustained growth: ~$240M
- Year 5 EBITDA at 18% mature margin: ~$43M
- Exit multiple (5 years out) at 4-5x revenue: ~$1.0-1.2B
- Plus 5 years of cumulative cash flow: ~$80-120M

Total 5-year value: ~$1.1-1.3B vs $600M acquisition. IRR ~12-15%, marginal but acceptable for strategic buyer.

### Integration risk

The dominant risk. Indie brand acquisitions usually fail when:
- Founder departs within 12-24 months
- Brand voice gets "professionalized" toward corporate norms
- Product launches slow from indie 6-month cycles to corporate 18-month cycles
- Channel mix shifts from DTC + specialty to mass (cheapens brand equity)

## Recommendation skeleton

**Recommendation:** **Acquire at $560M with structured retention + autonomy.** Negotiate $40M reduction from asking. Structure: $400M cash + $160M earn-out tied to revenue + brand-health KPIs over 4 years. Maintain target as autonomous operating unit; founder stays as CEO with 4-year retention package. Restrict channel expansion to specialty + premium retail (no mass channel for 5 years).

**Three supports:**

1. Valuation discount (7.5x → 7.0x revenue) reflects realistic integration risk.
2. Earn-out structure aligns founder incentives + protects Amalfi if brand declines post-acquisition.
3. Autonomy + channel restrictions prevent the typical post-acquisition brand erosion.

**One risk:** competing bidder may match or exceed our $560M offer. Mitigate by emphasizing structural advantages — Amalfi's distribution scale + autonomy commitment may be more attractive to the founder than a higher-price bid from a less compatible parent.

## What judges look for

- Did you apply the **M&A triad** (strategic / valuation / integration) systematically?
- Did you size **integration risk** as the dominant concern for indie beauty acquisitions?
- Did you propose **autonomy + earn-out** as deal structure rather than just negotiating price?
- Did you address **channel restriction** as a brand-protection mechanism?
