## The situation

Streaming subscriber growth has saturated across most developed markets. The growth playbook of the past decade (high content investment, low prices, rapid global rollout) is producing diminishing returns. Most major streamers (Netflix, Disney+, Max, Paramount+) have launched ad-supported tiers, raised prices, or both.

The strategic challenge: maintain revenue + subscriber growth without trapping the company in unsustainable content-spend escalation.

Three branches:

1. **Revenue lever options** — ad tier (lower-price + ad revenue), price increases, bundling, premium tiers.
2. **Content strategy** — invest more (compete on quality), invest less (sustainability), invest differently (genre focus, regional content).
3. **Cost structure** — content as largest cost, technology + customer acquisition + customer support.

## Key analysis

Comparing revenue strategies:

| Strategy | Revenue impact | Risk |
|----------|----------------|------|
| Ad-supported tier at $6 | Adds $3-5/sub via ads + grows subscribers | Cannibalizes premium subs |
| Price increase $15 → $18 | +10% revenue per sub | Drives churn |
| Bundle with broadband | Lower ARPU but stickier subs | Negotiation complexity |
| Content investment increase | Long-term sub retention | Margin compression now |

The non-obvious finding: **ad-tier launch is roughly accretive when properly executed** because:

- 50-70% of ad-tier subs are incremental (would not have subscribed at premium price)
- 25-35% downgrade from premium (cannibalization)
- 5-15% upgrade from ad-tier to premium over time

Net revenue per sub: ad-tier ($6 subscription + $4-6 ad ARPU = $10-12/month) vs premium ($15) — lower but with much larger addressable market.

## Recommendation skeleton

**Recommendation:** **Launch ad-supported tier + selective price increases.** Action 1: launch $6 ad-tier within 9 months — target 5M ad-tier subs in year 1, 15M by year 3. Action 2: raise standard tier $15 → $17, premium tier $20 → $24 — modest churn but offsetting revenue. Action 3: maintain content investment at current levels; defer aggressive new-investment until ad-tier matures. Bundle exploration with telco partners as opportunistic upside.

**Three supports:**

1. Ad-tier captures incremental customers + new revenue stream.
2. Price increases on existing tiers extract value from inelastic premium subs.
3. Holding content investment avoids accelerating an already-unsustainable spend trajectory.

**One risk:** ad-tier may cannibalize premium subs more than projected, hurting overall ARPU. Mitigate by designing ad-tier with meaningful limitations (content windowing, ads density) that preserve premium upgrade incentive.
