## The situation

Newspaper digital transformation has been one of the most-studied industry declines of the past two decades. Print revenue continues to decline structurally; digital revenue grows but at a slower rate than print declines. Most newspapers are managing decline rather than achieving growth.

The strategic options are limited: accelerate digital transition (at cost of remaining print profitability), harvest print + manage decline, find adjacent revenue streams (events, newsletters, services), consolidate via M&A.

Three branches:

1. **Print harvest economics** — how long can print remain profitable? What's the optimal decline curve?
2. **Digital revenue mix** — subscription growth potential, advertising sustainability, premium tier options.
3. **Adjacent revenue** — events, paid newsletters, services, content licensing, podcasts.

## Key analysis

Revenue trajectory for a typical regional newspaper:

| Year | Print | Digital sub | Digital ad | Adjacencies | Total |
|------|-------|-------------|------------|-------------|-------|
| Now | $110M | $50M | $40M | $0 | $200M |
| Year 3 | $65M | $70M | $45M | $10M | $190M |
| Year 5 | $35M | $85M | $50M | $30M | $200M |

The non-obvious move: **adjacencies are the swing factor.** Events ($5-15M for established regional papers), paid newsletters ($3-10M), and specialty content services (legal services, real estate, jobs) can collectively replace declining print revenue if developed deliberately.

For Clarion specifically:
- Print harvest: maintain print operations at managed decline, generate cash for 5-8 more years
- Digital subscription: invest in retention (engagement, exclusive content) to slow churn — incremental growth
- Adjacencies: launch 2-3 paid newsletter products + relaunch events business + explore specialty services

## Recommendation skeleton

**Recommendation:** **Three-track sustainability plan over 5 years.** Track 1: optimize print harvest — maintain print but accelerate cost reduction (printing consolidation, delivery rationalization). Track 2: digital subscription retention focus — invest in exclusive content + engagement features rather than top-of-funnel growth. Track 3: develop adjacencies — launch 3 paid newsletters by year 2, relaunch events business, explore specialty content services. Target: stable $190-210M revenue with growing digital + adjacency mix.

**Three supports:**

1. Print harvest extends the cash-engine longer rather than forcing premature transition.
2. Digital retention focus addresses subscriber base aging more effectively than growth marketing.
3. Adjacencies provide the structural new revenue needed for long-term sustainability.

**One risk:** adjacency businesses (events, newsletters) require capabilities Clarion doesn't have. Mitigate by hiring specialist teams + considering partnerships rather than building entirely internally.

## What judges look for

- Did you propose **multi-stream approach** rather than betting on single revenue source?
- Did you identify **adjacencies as swing factor** for long-term sustainability?
- Did you propose **digital retention focus** rather than continued growth marketing?
- Did you accept **print harvest as cash strategy** rather than rushing transition?
