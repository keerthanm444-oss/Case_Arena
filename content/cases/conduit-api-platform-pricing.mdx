## The situation

API platform pricing has unique dynamics: transaction-based pricing is industry standard, customers compare apples-to-apples on per-transaction cost, and the underlying network costs (payment rails, telecom, cloud infrastructure) are largely pass-through.

The strategic challenge: capturing more value when customer comparison is "what's my per-transaction cost" rather than "what's my platform value."

## Structuring

Three branches:

1. **Customer segmentation** — SMB vs mid-market vs enterprise. Each segment has different price elasticity, different value perception, different competitive alternatives.
2. **Cost structure** — what share of pricing is true value capture vs pass-through to payment networks? Customers usually focus on the all-in number.
3. **Pricing model options** — pure transaction (current), platform fee + transaction (decoupled), value-based (tied to specific outcomes).

## Key analysis

Customer-segment economics:

| Segment | LTV / CAC | Price elasticity | Switching cost |
|---------|-----------|------------------|----------------|
| SMB | 5-8x | High (>2.0) | Moderate |
| Mid-market | 8-12x | Medium (0.8-1.2) | High |
| Enterprise | 15-20x | Low (<0.5) | Very high |

The non-obvious move: **different pricing for different segments.**

- SMB are price-sensitive but high in count — pricing here should remain transaction-based, low friction.
- Enterprise are price-insensitive but few in number — they get personalized "platform agreements" with annual commitments + premium support.
- Mid-market is the strategic battleground — they have switching costs, value-perception is moderate, and they're the biggest segment by ARR contribution.

Two specific moves work for most customers in this position:

1. **Introduce a "platform fee" decoupled from transactions** for mid-market and enterprise. Frame it as paying for the software (uptime, security, compliance, developer experience) separate from the network pass-through. Customers psychologically separate "what we pay Conduit" from "what we pay the networks."

2. **Raise tier pricing at the high end** where elasticity is lowest. The bottom tier (SMB) stays unchanged; the top tier (enterprise) goes up 10-15% — mostly unnoticed.

A typical Conduit-like pricing migration captures 8-15% additional gross revenue with <2% churn impact, executed over 12-18 months.

## Recommendation skeleton

**Recommendation:** **Segmented pricing model.** Maintain transaction-only pricing for SMB (<$500K annual processing). Introduce a tiered platform fee for mid-market ($1K-5K/month + reduced transaction fee). Move enterprise to annual platform agreements with custom pricing. Roll out over 12 months, starting with new customers.

**Three supports:**

1. Each segment's elasticity + switching cost profile supports different pricing structures.
2. Decoupled platform fees clarify the "what's Conduit value" vs "what's network cost" mental model.
3. Phased rollout — new customers first, existing customers at contract renewal — minimizes churn risk.

**One risk:** existing customers may push back hard at renewal when seeing the new structure. Mitigate by structuring the new pricing to be roughly equivalent total cost for typical customer profiles at the time of transition, with the upside coming from larger or higher-value customers over time.

## What judges look for

- Did you **segment customers** rather than apply one pricing change uniformly?
- Did you identify **mid-market as the strategic value capture** segment?
- Did you propose **decoupled platform fee** to separate software value from network pass-through?
- Did you phase the rollout to **manage churn risk**?
