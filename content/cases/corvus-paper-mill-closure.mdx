## The situation

Paper-industry restructuring is one of the most-studied managed-decline cases of the past 20 years. Graphic paper demand is in structural decline (electronic substitution); packaging demand is growing (e-commerce, sustainable packaging). The strategic question is which assets to close, convert, or run for cash.

Different mills face very different futures depending on cost position, conversion potential, and regional context.

Three dimensions for each mill:

1. **Cost position** — where on the industry cost curve does this mill sit? Bottom-quartile cost mills should run regardless of product mix; top-quartile (worst cost) mills should close.
2. **Conversion feasibility** — can this mill convert graphic → packaging? Depends on machine configuration, fiber supply, regional packaging demand. Conversion capex is typically $200-500M per mill.
3. **Strategic role** — does this mill serve specific customers / regions that can't be served from elsewhere?

## Key analysis

A typical breakdown across 14 mills:

| Classification | Count | Action |
|---------------|-------|--------|
| Low-cost, packaging-capable | 3-4 | Keep + grow |
| Low-cost, graphic-only | 2-3 | Run for cash; close when demand falls below break-even |
| Mid-cost, conversion-feasible | 3-4 | **Convert to packaging** |
| Mid-cost, no conversion | 2-3 | Run for cash; close in 3-5 years |
| High-cost, any product | 2-3 | **Close immediately** |

The non-obvious finding: **mid-cost mills with conversion feasibility are the most valuable strategic assets.** Converting them captures the growing packaging market with existing infrastructure (saving 40-60% vs greenfield packaging capacity).

Conversion economics:

- Capex: $200-500M per mill
- Time to operation: 18-30 months
- Resulting cost position: typically improves by 10-20% vs pre-conversion (modern machinery, focused product mix)

The payback math: a $300M conversion that improves operating margin by $80M annually has a 3.75-year payback — strong if the underlying market growth holds.

## Recommendation skeleton

**Recommendation:** **Three-track strategy over 5 years.** Close 3 high-cost mills immediately (saves $120M annual operating loss). Convert 4 mid-cost graphic mills to packaging over 24-36 months ($1.4B capex). Run remaining 4 low-cost graphic mills for cash, closing one per year as graphic demand declines. Net portfolio in year 5: 7 mills (3 original packaging + 4 converted), all profitable.

**Three supports:**

1. Closure of high-cost mills stops the bleeding immediately without conversion risk.
2. Conversion of mid-cost mills captures packaging growth with strong payback economics.
3. Run-for-cash strategy on low-cost graphic mills monetizes remaining demand before final closure.

**One risk:** conversion projects often experience 30-50% capex overruns and 6-12 month delays. Mitigate by sequencing conversions (start with the simplest mill, build experience) and locking in major contractor relationships before kicking off.

## What judges look for

- Did you classify mills along **multiple dimensions** (cost, conversion, strategic role)?
- Did you identify **mid-cost convertible mills** as the most strategically valuable asset?
- Did you propose **simultaneous tracks** (close, convert, harvest) rather than treating uniformly?
- Did you size **conversion capex + payback economics** explicitly?
