## The situation

Pet food premiumization is one of the most studied growth trends in consumer goods over the past decade. The driver — pet humanization — is structural and continues. The challenge for incumbent mass/mid-market players: premium pet food often requires different manufacturing (fresh/refrigerated), different distribution (specialty pet, vet, DTC), and different brand equity than traditional kibble.

## Structuring

Three branches:

1. **Segment selection** — fresh/refrigerated (largest growth), raw (smaller niche), prescription/therapeutic (high-margin, slow growth). Different capital + capability requirements.
2. **Entry mode** — build organically, partner with a startup, acquire an existing player.
3. **Brand strategy** — extend Felicia brand into premium, launch new sub-brand, retain acquired brand identity.

## Key analysis

Comparing premium pet-food segments:

| Segment | Category growth | Manufacturing complexity | Distribution channel |
|---------|------------------|--------------------------|----------------------|
| Fresh/refrigerated | 20-30% YoY | High (cold chain) | DTC + premium grocery |
| Raw frozen | 15-20% | High (frozen logistics) | Specialty pet + DTC |
| Prescription | 5-10% | Medium | Vet channel |
| Premium dry/wet | 8-12% | Low (existing capability) | Specialty pet + premium grocery |

The non-obvious finding: **premium dry/wet is the natural extension** for a mass/mid-market kibble company. Fresh/refrigerated is operationally distant; acquisition is the only realistic path to scale there.

For Felicia specifically: organic premium dry/wet expansion captures meaningful share with existing capabilities. Fresh requires acquisition. Mixing strategies — build + acquire — is the realistic answer.

## Recommendation skeleton

**Recommendation:** **Two-track premium strategy.** Track 1: launch a premium dry/wet sub-brand within 12 months, investing $40M in product development + marketing — leveraging existing manufacturing + retail relationships. Track 2: acquire a $200-400M revenue fresh/refrigerated pet food player ($800M-1.5B acquisition; 24-36 month process). Retain acquired brand identity; Felicia parent invisible to consumer.

**Three supports:**

1. Premium dry/wet is achievable organically; building organically here is faster + cheaper than acquiring.
2. Fresh/refrigerated requires capabilities Felicia doesn't have — acquisition is the only credible path.
3. Sub-brand strategy preserves premium brand equity (mass-market parent isn't seen on premium products).

**One risk:** fresh pet food acquisition multiples are extremely high (15-25x EBITDA for category leaders). Mitigate with disciplined valuation — willing to wait for the right target rather than overpay; consider $50-150M tier of "challenger" fresh brands rather than only category leaders.

## What judges look for

- Did you separate **fresh/refrigerated** as operationally distinct from premium dry/wet?
- Did you propose **two tracks** (build + acquire) matched to capability fit?
- Did you size **sub-brand strategy** vs Felicia-brand extension?
- Did you address **acquisition multiple risk** in current pet-food M&A environment?
