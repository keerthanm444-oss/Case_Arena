## The situation

Airport capacity expansion is one of the largest, longest-payback infrastructure investments in transport. New terminals typically take 5-8 years to build, cost $2-6B, and depend on multi-decade passenger growth projections that are inherently uncertain. The pandemic period demonstrated that even "stable" passenger trajectories can shift dramatically.

The decision must balance multiple stakeholders: airlines (want more gates), travelers (want less congestion), local communities (want less noise + traffic), environmental advocates (want less aviation altogether).

Three branches:

1. **Demand realism** — passenger growth scenarios under different economic + environmental futures.
2. **Incremental alternatives** — gate optimization, off-peak scheduling, neighboring airport spillover, before committing to $4B build.
3. **Project economics** — payback structure, financing options, stakeholder agreement (airlines committing to use the terminal).

## Key analysis

Incremental capacity alternatives before new terminal:

| Option | Capacity gain | Capex | Time |
|--------|---------------|-------|------|
| Gate scheduling optimization | 5-10% | $5-20M | 12-18 months |
| Off-peak incentives + scheduling | 5-8% | $0 | 6-12 months |
| Existing terminal expansion | 10-15% | $200-500M | 24-36 months |
| New terminal | 40-45% | $4B | 60-84 months |

The non-obvious move: **exhaust incremental options before committing to new terminal.** Optimization + existing terminal expansion can add 20-30% capacity at <15% of new-terminal capex. This buys 7-10 years before a new terminal becomes essential.

For Gateway specifically: at 92% utilization, incremental options should be Phase 1. If utilization remains constrained after optimization, plan new terminal for delivery in years 6-8 — not committing capex today.

## Recommendation skeleton

**Recommendation:** **Phased capacity approach.** Phase 1 (years 1-3): aggressive incremental optimization + existing terminal expansion ($300-500M capex; +20-25% effective capacity). Phase 2 (years 3-5): re-evaluate passenger growth trajectory + community engagement. Phase 3 (years 5-10): new terminal if demand justifies, conditional on airline commitments to specific gate utilization + environmental mitigations.

**Three supports:**

1. Incremental options deliver meaningful capacity at much lower capital risk.
2. Phased decision-making preserves optionality if demand changes.
3. Airline commitment to gate utilization reduces stranded-asset risk on new terminal.

**One risk:** delay may aggravate airline + traveler frustration, potentially shifting traffic to competing airports. Mitigate by transparent communication of phased plan + visible incremental improvements.

## What judges look for

- Did you propose **incremental options before new terminal**?
- Did you size **demand uncertainty** as the largest project risk?
- Did you propose **airline commitments** as risk-transfer mechanism?
- Did you address **community + environmental opposition** as binding constraints?
