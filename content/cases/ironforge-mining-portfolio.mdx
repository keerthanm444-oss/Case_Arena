## The situation

Mining company transitions out of thermal coal have been a defining theme of the past decade. The drivers — climate policy, financing constraints (banks won't fund coal expansion), customer (utility) decline — are structural. Most major miners have committed to coal exit, with the strategic question being how quickly + what to pivot to.

Critical minerals (copper, lithium, nickel, cobalt) offer the most-cited transition path. Demand is growing 4-10% annually driven by EVs + renewable infrastructure. Multiples for critical-minerals assets are high but justified by demand outlook.

Three branches:

1. **Thermal coal harvest** — how long can existing coal operations continue profitably? Decommissioning timeline?
2. **Met coal positioning** — steel decarbonization will reduce met coal demand long-term but more slowly than thermal. Met coal can be cash engine for 10-15 more years.
3. **Critical minerals entry** — organic exploration + brownfield expansion vs acquisition. Geography + capability mapping.

## Key analysis

A typical pattern for a thermal-coal-heavy miner transitioning:

| Asset class | Strategic role |
|-------------|----------------|
| Thermal coal | Harvest aggressively; close worst mines, run best for 10-15 years |
| Met coal | Operate; treat as transitioning cash engine |
| Critical minerals | Growth platform; build via acquisition + brownfield |
| Other commodities | Evaluate case-by-case |

The non-obvious move: **harvest met coal aggressively in years 1-5** when steel demand remains. Met coal generates much higher margins than thermal coal during the same period; using met-coal cash flow to fund critical-minerals acquisitions accelerates the transition.

For Ironforge specifically:
- Thermal coal: harvest, close 30-40% of operations over 5 years
- Met coal: maintain + selectively grow (steel industry won't fully transition for 15-20 years)
- Critical minerals: aggressive acquisition program ($3-5B over 5-7 years) targeting copper + lithium assets in our operational regions

## Recommendation skeleton

**Recommendation:** **Three-track portfolio transition over 10 years.** Track 1: harvest thermal coal — close 30-40% of mines, operate remainder until economically uncompetitive. Track 2: maintain + selectively expand met coal as transition cash engine. Track 3: aggressive critical-minerals expansion — $3-4B in acquisitions + $1B brownfield over 7 years targeting copper (60%), lithium (25%), nickel (15%). Target end-state: <20% thermal coal revenue, 30-40% critical minerals.

**Three supports:**

1. Multi-asset approach uses met-coal + thermal-coal cash flow to fund critical-minerals build.
2. Critical-minerals demand outlook supports premium acquisition multiples.
3. Phased transition avoids stranding capital in declining assets while building positions for growth.

**One risk:** critical-minerals prices may correct sharply (commodity cycles), compressing acquisition economics. Mitigate by patient deployment — willing to wait for cycle troughs to make major acquisitions; not all-in in any single year.

## What judges look for

- Did you separate **thermal coal (decline)** from **met coal (transitioning cash engine)**?
- Did you propose **specific critical-minerals targets** rather than treating as generic?
- Did you use **coal cash flow to fund transition** rather than external capital?
- Did you address **commodity-cycle risk** in critical-minerals acquisitions?
