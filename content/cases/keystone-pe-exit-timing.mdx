## The situation

PE exit timing combines several distinct considerations: company-specific value creation runway, M&A market conditions, fund timeline (LP commitments), buyer-set availability. The standard framework — exit when forward IRR drops below required return — is right but requires careful calibration of each input.

The honest assessment: holding for additional EBITDA growth requires confidence the multiple won't compress; selling now locks in known multiple at the cost of foregone growth.

## Structuring

Three branches:

1. **Value-creation runway** — what specific operational improvements remain? Are they achievable, and how much value would they create?
2. **Market timing** — what does the current M&A environment look like for the asset? Multiples, buyer activity, transaction volume.
3. **Forward IRR scenarios** — for each exit timing option, what's the expected IRR? Risk-adjusted comparison is the decision basis.

## Key analysis

Working through the three scenarios:

**(a) Exit now:**
- Likely outcome: $1.5B exit value, ~2.0x cash multiple over 4 years, ~19% IRR
- Certainty: high (M&A market is functional; multiples are at reasonable levels)
- Risk: leaving 20-30% of potential value on the table if hold-scenario plays out well

**(b) Hold 2 more years:**
- Target outcome: $2.0B exit value, ~2.7x cash multiple over 6 years, ~18% IRR
- Required assumptions: EBITDA grows 25-30%, multiple stays at ~11x
- Risk: multiple compression (could end up at 9-10x in 2 years, giving back gains)

**(c) Recapitalization:**
- Returns ~$400M to LPs now via debt
- Holds for 4 more years
- IRR can be higher due to capital efficiency
- Adds leverage + execution risk

The non-obvious finding: **the IRR is roughly equal between exit-now and hold-2-years**, but exit-now has much lower variance. The hold scenario depends on M&A market conditions in 24 months, which are unpredictable.

A typical pattern from PE funds at year 4-5: the EBITDA growth they expected to capture in the final hold years is often offset by multiple compression, leading to similar total proceeds but with more risk.

## Recommendation skeleton

**Recommendation:** **Exit now via dual-track process.** Run a structured process (8-10 strategic + 4-6 PE buyers) over the next 6 months. Target close at ~$1.5-1.6B. Use a dual-track (strategic auction + IPO option) to maximize price competition. Avoid recap — adds leverage to an asset already at PE-standard 5-6x debt-to-EBITDA.

**Three supports:**

1. Forward IRRs are roughly equal across scenarios; lower-variance option wins.
2. M&A market conditions are functional now; risk of deterioration in 24 months is non-trivial.
3. Dual-track maximizes the realized multiple within the current market window.

**One risk:** the M&A process may not generate sufficient buyer competition; back-up plan is needed. Mitigate by pre-marketing to top strategic candidates 90 days before formal launch, gauging interest before committing to the process.

## What judges look for

- Did you analyze **forward IRR for each timing scenario** rather than guessing?
- Did you identify **multiple compression risk** as the key variable for the hold scenario?
- Did you propose **a dual-track exit process** to maximize realized value?
- Did you reject **recapitalization** despite its appeal as a "have it both ways" option?
