## The situation

Medicare Advantage is the fastest-growing segment of US health insurance (~9% YoY membership growth historically). Its economics are different from commercial insurance: per-member-per-month payments from CMS based on risk-adjusted scores, STAR ratings driving bonus payments, MLR rules constraining the profit margin.

At 80K members, Meridiem is sub-scale for MA economics. The fixed costs of MA operations (care management infrastructure, STAR rating teams, network management) require ~250K+ members to spread efficiently. The question is whether to invest the capital to get there.

Three branches:

1. **Market attractiveness** — MA TAM in our footprint, growth rate, regulatory tailwinds. Generally favorable.
2. **Our ability to win** — STAR ratings (need 4.0+ to compete), network adequacy, care management capability, brand resonance with Medicare-eligible population.
3. **Path + cost** — organic growth (slow, requires marketing + member acquisition), acquisition (faster, requires significant capital), JV with a larger national player (lower capital, lower control).

## Key analysis

The pivotal economic question: **at what scale does MA break even, and how long does it take to get there?**

A typical scale-economics curve:

- 80K members (current): -3% to -5% margin
- 150K members: -1% to +1% margin (breakeven crossing)
- 300K members: +3% to +5% margin (target margin range)
- 500K members: +5% to +7% margin (mature scale)

Organic growth from 80K to 500K is typically 15-20% YoY growth, achievable but tight on the 5-year timeline. Acquisition can accelerate but most regional MA assets aren't actively for sale; auctions get expensive.

Two non-obvious constraints:

- **STAR ratings determine bonus payments.** Plans below 4.0 STAR cannot grow profitably even with members. Investment in care management + member experience precedes any scale plan.
- **Network adequacy regulations** require provider contracting in every county served. Geographic expansion isn't just marketing; it's network build.

## Recommendation skeleton

**Recommendation:** Scale MA, but on a 7-year timeline with 4.0 STAR achievement as a precondition. Year 1: invest $40M in care management + STAR rating infrastructure. Year 2-3: organic growth in existing markets to ~200K members. Year 4-5: opportunistic acquisition or geographic expansion to 350K+. Year 6-7: scale to 500K if STAR + economics support.

**Three supports:**

1. The economics support scale, but only if STAR ratings improve first — operationally, achieving 4.0 is the binding constraint.
2. 7 years is more realistic than 5 — the original CEO target understates the time required for STAR improvement + sub-scale to mature-scale transition.
3. Capital deployment phasing limits downside while preserving optionality on faster paths if STAR improvement happens ahead of plan.

**One risk:** CMS may change MA reimbursement methodology unfavorably, compressing economics across the industry. Mitigate by maintaining the current commercial + Medicaid lines as ballast and avoiding all-in commitment to MA.

## What judges look for

- Did you recognize **scale economics + STAR ratings as joint constraints**? Either alone doesn't capture the picture.
- Did you reject the 5-year CEO timeline and propose 7+ years as more realistic?
- Did you identify **care management investment as the precondition** for growth, not the result of it?
- Did you discuss **acquisition vs organic** carefully without defaulting to either?
