## The situation

Established CPG brands facing better-for-you challengers is one of the most-studied competitive dynamics in food + beverage. The pattern: incumbents lose 3-8% category share over 3-5 years, eventually respond with sub-brands or acquisitions. The companies that respond early (sub-brand or acquisition before share loss reaches 10%) usually retain category leadership; those that delay often lose the franchise permanently.

Three branches:

1. **Threat assessment** — is the 4% share loss stable or accelerating? At what point does the challenger become an existential threat vs a niche?
2. **Response options** — build sub-brand (~$80-150M, 18-24 months to scale), acquire ($400-600M, 6-12 months), reformulate (low cost, high risk of alienating core users).
3. **Brand strategy** — single-brand portfolio or multi-brand portfolio with distinct positioning?

## Key analysis

Comparing response options:

**Build sub-brand:**
- Time to scale: 24-36 months for credible presence
- Capital: $100-200M total investment over 3 years
- Risk: building a new credible better-for-you brand against established challengers is hard; brand-credibility advantage usually goes to first movers

**Acquire competitor:**
- Time to scale: immediate (acquired revenue contributes day 1)
- Capital: $400-600M
- Risk: acquired brand often loses momentum post-deal (the "kill the indie" pattern); requires careful integration

**Reformulate core brands:**
- Time: 12-18 months
- Capital: $30-50M (reformulation + marketing)
- Risk: alienates current users without winning new ones; "trying to be both" brand positioning is often the worst outcome

The non-obvious finding: **reformulating core brands is usually the worst option** despite being the cheapest. Better-for-you consumers don't trust legacy brand reformulations; legacy consumers feel betrayed by reformulation. The right answer is multi-brand strategy.

Between build and acquire: acquisition shortcuts time but at high multiple. For Orchid specifically, the calculation depends on how fast the challenger is growing — if 30%+ YoY, acquisition multiple is justified; if 10-15%, building may be cheaper.

## Recommendation skeleton

**Recommendation:** **Acquire the better-for-you competitor at $480M with structured retention.** Maintain target as distinct brand portfolio (no integration into Orchid umbrella). Founder stays 3+ years with retention package. Parallel: defer reformulation of core brands; protect legacy users. Total investment: $480M acquisition + $80M operational support over 3 years.

**Three supports:**

1. Acquisition captures momentum that organic build cannot match in time.
2. Brand independence preserves the indie credibility that drove the challenger's growth.
3. Avoiding core-brand reformulation prevents alienating Orchid's legacy customer base.

**One risk:** integration may erode the acquired brand's challenger credibility over time. Mitigate with strict operating-model autonomy + brand-architecture rules (no Orchid logo, no shared marketing).

## What judges look for

- Did you reject **reformulating core brands** as the worst option?
- Did you size **acquisition multiple** against organic-build economics?
- Did you propose **separate brand portfolio** rather than integration?
- Did you identify the **founder retention** as the integration linchpin?
