## The situation

Regional banking ROE compression is one of the most-studied financial services patterns of the past 15 years. The structural drivers are well understood: digital channel migration reducing branch traffic, fintech competition for non-interest income, regulatory cost increases, low-rate environment compressing margins.

For most regional banks, the gap to peers comes from a combination of (1) under-monetized customer relationships and (2) over-invested branch infrastructure.

Three branches:

1. **Net interest margin** — usually in line with peers for similar-sized banks; not the main driver.
2. **Non-interest income** — fees, wealth management, treasury services, card interchange. Often the largest variance vs peers.
3. **Cost structure** — branch network, IT infrastructure, regulatory compliance, headcount.

## Key analysis

For Pioneer specifically, the 65% cost-to-income vs peer 55% suggests $400M+ of excess cost annually. Decomposing that gap:

| Cost category | Pioneer vs peer | Gap |
|---------------|-----------------|-----|
| Branch network | Higher branch count per $B assets | $150-200M |
| IT / legacy systems | Higher than peers, ongoing modernization | $80-120M |
| Headcount (back office) | Higher than peers | $60-100M |
| Risk + compliance | In line | Minimal |

The non-interest income gap is typically driven by:

- Underpenetrated wealth management (~30-50% of customers eligible but not enrolled)
- Lower interchange income (legacy debit card relationships at lower rates)
- Underutilized treasury services for commercial customers

The combined gap analysis suggests ROE could improve to 11-13% within 36 months if both cost reduction and non-interest income growth execute well.

The non-obvious move: **the branch network is the largest single lever** — but closures are politically painful for a regional bank with customer loyalty tied to local presence. Phased branch consolidation with strong digital migration support is the only way.

## Recommendation skeleton

**Recommendation:** **Three-track ROE improvement over 36 months.** Track 1: close 15-20% of branches in over-banked metros + invest digital experience (saves $80-120M annually). Track 2: roll out wealth management to top 30% of customers (adds $60-80M non-interest income). Track 3: legacy IT modernization (saves $50-80M annually starting year 2). Combined target: 12% ROE by year 3.

**Three supports:**

1. Branch + IT cost gap is the largest opportunity, but execution requires multi-year commitment.
2. Non-interest income growth (wealth, treasury) leverages existing customer relationships at high marginal margin.
3. Multi-track approach distributes execution risk; no single initiative carries the entire ROE improvement target.

**One risk:** branch closures may drive customer attrition in tight regional markets. Mitigate by closing only in metros with peer branches nearby; maintain branch presence in single-bank-town markets where attrition risk is highest.

## What judges look for

- Did you **decompose ROE gap by component** (NIM, non-interest income, cost) rather than treating as monolithic?
- Did you identify **branch network as the largest single cost lever** while recognizing the political constraints?
- Did you size **non-interest income growth opportunities** (wealth, treasury) specifically?
- Did you propose a **multi-track plan** with realistic 36-month timeline?
