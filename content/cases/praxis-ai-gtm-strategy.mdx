## The situation

GTM strategy at 18-month, $0-ARR stage is one of the most consequential decisions for a developer-tools startup. The runway constraint (12 months) forces choices: enterprise sales requires 18-month cycles, PLG requires top-of-funnel scale that takes time to build, community-first requires deep technical content and patience.

The right answer depends entirely on who the user is and how they buy.

Three branches:

1. **User reality** — solo developer or part of an engineering org? Does the user have purchasing authority?
2. **GTM economics** — for each path, what's the CAC, time-to-revenue, sales-team build cost?
3. **Runway compatibility** — does this GTM produce revenue within 12 months? If not, it's not feasible given current funding.

## Key analysis

Walking each option:

**(a) PLG with self-serve paid tier:**
- Pricing: $20-50/user/month for individual, $50-100/user/month for team plans
- Conversion: 2-5% of free users to paid (industry benchmark)
- Time to revenue: immediate (self-serve)
- CAC: ~$20-50 per user via content + community marketing
- Need ~50K free users to generate $1M ARR — achievable from current 2K base in 6-9 months at growth rates this kind of product can support
- Risk: PLG hits a ceiling at ~$50-100M ARR; enterprise expansion needed eventually anyway

**(b) Sales-led enterprise:**
- Pricing: $50-200K ACV
- Sales cycle: 6-12 months
- Time to first revenue: 8-12 months at best — close to or beyond runway
- CAC: $50-150K per closed deal
- Risk: runway might not allow first deals to close; capital-intensive

**(c) Developer marketing + community-first:**
- Build technical content, conferences, free tier
- Time to revenue: 12-18 months indirect (via PLG conversion)
- CAC: low per user but slow ramp
- Risk: too slow for current runway

The PLG path is the only one compatible with the 12-month runway constraint. The strategic question becomes how to enable enterprise expansion later, not whether to start enterprise-first.

## Recommendation skeleton

**Recommendation:** **PLG with self-serve paid tier**, launched within 60 days. Pricing: $30/user/month individual, $25/user/month for teams (5+ seats). Focus on team-plan conversion as the primary growth lever. At month 9, evaluate whether team-plan customers are graduating to enterprise needs (SSO, billing consolidation) — if yes, hire first 2 enterprise AEs to upsell.

**Three supports:**

1. PLG is the only GTM compatible with 12-month runway — sales-led can't close first deals fast enough.
2. Team-plan pricing creates a natural pull toward larger account expansion (the path to enterprise).
3. Enterprise hire timing is gated to demand signal, not speculation.

**One risk:** developer-tools PLG is increasingly competitive; differentiation against GitHub Copilot + Cursor requires sharp positioning. Mitigate by leading with the specific use case (AI code review) rather than the general "AI for developers" category.

## What judges look for

- Did you tie GTM choice to **the runway constraint** explicitly?
- Did you size **PLG conversion math** (free user count × conversion rate) rather than waving at growth?
- Did you reject **enterprise-first** for runway reasons, even though it's tempting for the ACV?
- Did you propose **enterprise expansion gated to demand signal**, not committed up front?
