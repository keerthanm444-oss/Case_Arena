## The situation

Out-of-home (OOH) advertising digital conversion has been one of the highest-ROI capital deployment opportunities in advertising over the past decade. Digital units run multiple ad rotations (5-15 advertisers per location vs 1 for static), command higher CPMs, and enable dynamic targeting (time-of-day, weather, location-specific).

The constraints are physical (limited prime locations), regulatory (municipalities limit digital density), and capital (conversion is expensive).

Three branches:

1. **Location quality tier** — top tier (high traffic, urban core), mid tier (mid-density), bottom tier (low traffic).
2. **Regulatory environment** — by municipality, what's the digital permit availability + restrictions?
3. **Conversion economics** — payback per location type.

## Key analysis

Per-location conversion economics:

| Location tier | Conversion capex | Annual revenue lift | Payback |
|---------------|------------------|---------------------|---------|
| Top tier (urban high-traffic) | $100-150K | $80-150K | 1-2 years |
| Mid tier | $70-100K | $40-70K | 2-4 years |
| Bottom tier | $50-70K | $15-30K | 3-5 years |

The non-obvious finding: **top-tier digital conversion has spectacular payback (1-2 years)**, but conversion supply is limited by regulatory + permitting constraints. Mid-tier payback is good (2-4 years); bottom-tier is marginal.

For Skyline specifically: aggressive conversion of top-tier locations (~1,800 of the 12,000) is the priority. Mid-tier conversion follows opportunistically based on demand + permit availability. Bottom-tier should remain static.

## Recommendation skeleton

**Recommendation:** **Tiered conversion strategy over 5 years.** Phase 1: convert top-tier 1,800 locations ($240M capex; 1-2 year payback per location). Phase 2: mid-tier 2,000 locations based on permit availability + demand ($170M). Phase 3: bottom-tier mostly remains static — selective conversion only with clear demand commitment. End state: 32% digital (3,800 of 12,000), $450-700M annual revenue increase.

**Three supports:**

1. Top-tier conversion captures the highest-ROI opportunities first.
2. Tiered approach avoids overinvesting in locations with poor payback economics.
3. Regulatory + permit constraints limit conversion pace regardless of capital availability.

**One risk:** competitors may secure prime digital permits in target markets before Skyline acts. Mitigate by aggressive permitting activity now — secure rights even before construction.

## What judges look for

- Did you **tier locations by economics** rather than converting uniformly?
- Did you identify **top-tier conversion** as highest-priority?
- Did you address **regulatory constraints** as a binding factor?
- Did you propose **selective bottom-tier conversion** based on demand?
