## The situation

The "squeezed middle" pattern in consumer goods is well-documented. Mid-tier brands face structural pressure from premium players (technology leadership + brand prestige) and budget players (cost leadership + retail distribution). The strategic options are limited: defend (rarely sustainable), abandon (loses scale + retail relationships), or differentiate into a defensible niche.

Three branches:

1. **Mid-tier viability** — at 12% margin, is the business making money on a fully-loaded basis? Below cost of capital usually means slow decline.
2. **Strategic options** — exit (focus on premium + budget), refresh (more differentiated product), reposition (audiophile niche), maintain (status quo).
3. **Operational interdependence** — does mid-tier share R&D, manufacturing, or sales force with premium/budget? Exit affects more than mid-tier P&L.

## Key analysis

The diagnostic question: **what does the mid-tier customer journey look like?**

Two patterns commonly appear:

- **Trade-up customer:** starts mid-tier, upgrades to premium over time. In this case, mid-tier is a funnel for premium sales; exiting loses the funnel.
- **Stuck-mid customer:** stays in mid-tier indefinitely. No funnel value; mid-tier must justify on standalone economics.

For Sonance specifically, the funnel hypothesis is testable from customer data. If 20%+ of premium customers were previously mid-tier customers, mid-tier has strategic value beyond standalone margin.

The non-obvious move: **redefine mid-tier as "Sonance entry point" rather than competing head-on with category leaders.** Position mid-tier products to share design language + technology with premium (creating "Sonance look + sound at half the price") rather than competing with Apple/Sony on features.

This works only if Sonance has genuine premium-tier credibility. Without it, repositioning mid-tier as premium-light is hollow.

## Recommendation skeleton

**Recommendation:** **Refresh mid-tier as "Sonance entry experience"** rather than exit. Reduce SKU count from 12 → 5 (kill underperformers). Redesign remaining 5 to share design language + key technology with premium tier. Position pricing at $200-280 (slight increase from current mid). Concurrently invest R&D in flagship premium product to reinforce overall brand credibility. Don't exit mid-tier despite margin pressure.

**Three supports:**

1. Mid-tier serves as premium-tier funnel; exiting reduces premium-tier customer pipeline.
2. SKU rationalization recovers some margin without abandoning the tier.
3. Sharing design + tech with premium leverages R&D investment across more revenue base.

**One risk:** mid-tier margin may continue compressing despite refresh. Mitigate by setting explicit 24-month milestones (margin back to 18%+, growth resumed) — if not achieved, escalate to selective exit.

## What judges look for

- Did you analyze **mid-tier funnel value** beyond standalone margin?
- Did you reject **full exit** in favor of repositioning?
- Did you propose **shared R&D with premium** as the differentiation mechanism?
- Did you set **explicit milestones** for re-evaluating the strategy?
