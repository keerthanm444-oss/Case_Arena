## The situation

State revenue agencies face common challenges: legacy IT systems, manual auditing, limited data integration, increasing tax gap from underreported income + sales tax evasion. Modernization investments typically pay back 5-10x via increased collection — but require multi-year transformation programs.

The $500M target over 3 years is roughly 20% of the estimated tax gap — aggressive but achievable through analytics + automation.

Three branches:

1. **Tax gap diagnosis** — by tax type, taxpayer segment, evasion mechanism (underreporting, non-filing, fraud).
2. **Capability gaps** — data analytics, audit selection, automated matching, fraud detection.
3. **Investment priorities** — technology, audit workforce, taxpayer experience.

## Key analysis

Typical tax-gap composition:

| Source | Share of gap | Detection difficulty |
|--------|--------------|----------------------|
| Underreported business income (small biz, cash) | 40-50% | High — needs analytics + audit |
| Sales tax under-remittance | 15-25% | Medium — needs data matching |
| Non-filing (failed to file at all) | 10-20% | Low — data matching identifies |
| Misclassification (employees as 1099) | 5-15% | Medium |
| Other (fraud, errors) | 5-10% | Variable |

The non-obvious finding: **non-filing recovery is the highest-ROI starting point.** Identifying taxpayers who should have filed but didn't is straightforward via data matching with IRS, employer reports, banking data. Recovery rates approach 60-70%. No need for complex audit.

For the state specifically:
- Non-filing: $200-400M gap, 60% recoverable in 18 months via data matching
- Underreported business income: $1-1.5B gap, 15-20% recoverable via analytics-driven audit
- Sales tax: $300-600M, 30-40% recoverable via marketplace + payment data

Combined potential: $500-700M over 3 years — achievable.

## Recommendation skeleton

**Recommendation:** **Three-track modernization over 3 years.** Track 1: deploy non-filing data matching system within 12 months ($150M one-time + $20M annual; $200M annual recovery). Track 2: implement analytics-driven audit selection — replace random sampling with risk-based ($80M investment; $200-300M annual recovery). Track 3: marketplace data integration for sales tax ($60M; $100-150M annual recovery). Total investment $290M; recovery $500-650M annually by year 3.

**Three supports:**

1. Non-filing track has highest ROI + fastest payback — start there to fund subsequent investments.
2. Analytics-driven audit captures the largest gap category (business income underreporting).
3. Marketplace integration addresses the structural sales tax gap from digital commerce.

**One risk:** legacy auditor resistance to analytics-driven workflows may slow execution. Mitigate by training-first approach + retaining manual override authority while building analytics confidence.

## What judges look for

- Did you **decompose tax gap** by source rather than treating it monolithically?
- Did you identify **non-filing detection** as highest-ROI starting point?
- Did you propose **analytics-driven audit** rather than just more auditors?
- Did you size **investment per track** with realistic payback economics?
