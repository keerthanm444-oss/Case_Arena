## The situation

Podcast industry economics have shifted significantly over the past 5 years. The 2019-2022 "podcast gold rush" (Spotify acquisitions of Gimlet, The Ringer, Megaphone for >$500M combined) drove valuations to historic highs. Subsequent industry adjustment (Spotify layoffs, deal cancellations, multiple compression) has brought multiples back to more reasonable levels.

The strategic landscape: major platforms (Spotify, iHeart, SiriusXM) consolidating; independent networks selling or struggling; top podcasters increasingly capable of going independent.

Three branches:

1. **Organic growth** — increase ARPU on existing shows (better ad sales, programmatic), launch new shows, deepen existing audiences.
2. **M&A growth** — acquire competing networks at current multiples (3-5x revenue).
3. **Subscription growth** — launch ad-free or premium content tier.

## Key analysis

Comparing growth paths:

| Path | 3-year impact | Capital required | Risk |
|------|---------------|------------------|------|
| Organic (ARPU + new shows) | $80M → $150M | $30-50M | Moderate |
| M&A (acquire 2-3 networks) | $80M → $250M | $250-400M | High (integration + valuation) |
| Subscription tier | $80M → $130M | $30M | Moderate-high (adoption uncertainty) |
| Combined approach | $80M → $250-300M | $300-450M | Moderate |

The non-obvious finding: **podcast M&A multiples have come down materially from peak**, making selective acquisitions more attractive than 18-24 months ago. Networks that previously sold for 6-8x revenue are now in the 3-5x range.

For Vellum specifically: a combined strategy — moderate M&A (1-2 acquisitions at attractive multiples) + organic ARPU improvement — produces the best risk-adjusted growth. Subscription tier is unlikely to be transformative; podcast audiences have been resistant to paid subscription except for very specific premium content.

## Recommendation skeleton

**Recommendation:** **Combined M&A + organic growth strategy over 3 years.** Track 1: acquire 1-2 mid-sized podcast networks ($150-250M; 3-4x revenue multiples) — adds $50-100M revenue + portfolio depth. Track 2: improve ARPU on existing portfolio (better ad sales operation, programmatic integration) — $13 ARPU to $18-20. Track 3: limited subscription tier focused on bonus content for fans of top shows. Target revenue: $250-280M by year 3 (3x+ growth).

**Three supports:**

1. Multi-track approach balances M&A risk with organic improvement.
2. Current M&A multiples support value-creating acquisitions; window may close as industry recovers.
3. ARPU improvement is achievable on existing portfolio without major new investments.

**One risk:** podcast advertising market may compress further, reducing both ARPU + acquisition target economics. Mitigate by phasing M&A — close one deal first, validate economics, then pursue second.
