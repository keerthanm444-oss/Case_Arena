## The situation

Auto rental is one of the most asset-intensive transport businesses. Returns depend on three interlocking variables: fleet size (capital tied up), utilization (revenue per asset), and residual value (depreciation expense). Optimization across all three requires fleet decisions to match demand patterns by location, time, and vehicle type.

<IssueTreeEmbed slug="velocity-tree" height={400} />

Three branches:

1. **Fleet size + composition** — total fleet by location, by vehicle type. Right-sizing to demand patterns.
2. **Holding period** — when to sell vehicles. Earlier sales = lower depreciation but higher fleet rotation cost.
3. **Pricing + utilization** — revenue management, dynamic pricing, customer segment mix.

## Key analysis

Fleet optimization levers:

| Lever | Impact on margin | Implementation difficulty |
|-------|------------------|---------------------------|
| Right-size fleet by location | +2-4 ppts | Medium — requires demand modeling |
| Shorten holding period (sell at 18-24 mo vs 28-32) | +1-2 ppts | Low — operational change |
| Vehicle mix shift toward higher-revenue | +1-3 ppts | Medium — requires demand validation |
| Revenue management improvements | +1-2 ppts | Medium — pricing system upgrade |

The non-obvious finding: **shortening holding period is the easiest immediate margin lever.** Selling vehicles at 18-24 months captures higher residual values + reduces maintenance cost on aging fleet.

For Velocity specifically:
- Current utilization 78% vs peer 82% — suggests fleet is 5-7% oversized at some locations
- Holding period likely 28-32 months — shortening to 22-24 captures 1-2 ppts margin
- Revenue per day $6 below peers — partially fleet mix, partially pricing optimization

## Recommendation skeleton

**Recommendation:** **Multi-lever fleet optimization over 18 months.** Action 1: reduce fleet at low-utilization locations by 5-8% (~10,000 vehicles); shift to high-demand markets. Action 2: shorten average holding period to 22-24 months (faster turnover, higher residual capture). Action 3: vehicle mix shift toward SUVs + crossovers (higher revenue + competitive demand). Target operating margin improvement of 4-6 percentage points within 18 months.

**Three supports:**

1. Fleet right-sizing by location addresses the utilization gap directly.
2. Shorter holding period is a low-implementation-risk margin lever.
3. Mix shift toward in-demand vehicle types captures revenue per day improvement.

**One risk:** used-car market may soften unexpectedly, reducing residual values. Mitigate by maintaining flexible holding period — willing to extend if residuals collapse rather than locking in losses.

## What judges look for

- Did you propose **multiple coordinated levers** rather than single solution?
- Did you identify **holding period** as a low-friction lever?
- Did you propose **location-specific fleet sizing** rather than uniform changes?
- Did you address **used-car market risk** as exogenous variable?
